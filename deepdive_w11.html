Geography 485L Deep Dive Page

Week 11 April 2, 2014

Question 1

What is the high-level description of the problem/question you want to help answer through the presentation of a collection of geographic data over the internet?

I'd like to take a look at the distribution of earthquakes in New Mexico compared to the locations of oil and gas wells.

Question 2

Who is the target audience for the information you want to provide?

My target audience is any concerned party. Many communities are considering or have already implemented fracking bans due to environmental concerns. (Mora County, NM)

Question 3

What geographic region does your problem area represent? Please describe it in words (e.g. New Mexico, Alberta Canada, etc.) and define it in terms of a geographic (WGS84) (latitude and longitude) bounding box.

Some of my data covers the United States, and some is just New Mexico. There are clusters of oil and gas wells in New Mexico, so I will focus on either the area near Farmingon, NM or Carlsbad, NM.

Question 4

What types of data do you want to include in your project? Include a description (data content: e.g. elevation data, hydrographic survey, etc.) and types (i.e. raster, vector).

I've got point location data for wells, earthquakes, and 2008 data for aquifer contamination events. I also have boundary files for watersheds, shale plays and shale basins.

Question 5

What projection will you use for the presentation of your project data? Again, describe it, provide your reasoning for selection, and provide the corresponding EPSG code.

I will use WGS 84/EPSG:4326. Most of my data is already in this datum.

Question 6

Where do you anticipate acquiring data for the project from?

I acquired data from RGIS, the USGS Earthquake Archive, and FracTracker.

Question 7

What barriers to acquiring and processing the needed data do you anticipate?

Drilling operations have to disclose chemical use, and can submit this information to a national registry called FracFocus. State regulatory agencies also have data about specific wells, but generally you can only access the information one well at a time. I thought it might be difficult and time consuming to collect the data for the entire state, but I discovered a non-profit called SkyTruth had already gathered the data for the entire US, and provided it to FracTracker.
