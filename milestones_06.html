Geography 485L Milestone Page

Week 6 February 26, 2014

Question 1:

What search engine did you use?

Google

Question 2:

How many ‘hits’ did you get?

About 324,000 results for the “SERVICE=WMS” search, and 61,100 results “REQUEST=GetCapabilities”.

Question 3:

How useful (generally in terms of getting a pointers to live WMS services [defined as a functioning GetCapabilities request]) were the ‘hits’? Pick two of the services that included live "GetCapabilities" requests that you found above, and answer the following questions about each.

  • What is the URL for the full GetCapabilities request to the service?
  • What is the Name of the service?
  • What Format(s) are available for GetMap requests from the service?
  • How many layers are included in the service (including nesting layers)?

Neither of these search terms produced many actual live WMS services. I mostly found more information about composing a GetCapabilities request, and found lists of services.

Service 1:

  • http://onmars.jpl.nasa.gov/wms.cgi?map=Mars&request=GetCapabilities&version=1.1.1
  • Name: OGS:WMS, Title: JPL Planetary Map Service
  • image/jpeg, image/png, image/tiff, image/geotiff
  • 5 layers

Question 4:

  • What is the URL for the full GetCapabilities request to the service?
  • What is the Name of the service?
  • What Format(s) are available for GetMap requests from the service?
  • How many layers are included in the service (including nesting layers)?

Service 2:

  • http://www.mapaplanet.org/explorer-bin/imageMaker.cgi?map=Moon&request=GetCapabilities&version=1.1.1
  • Name: OGS:WMS, Title: USGS Astrogeology Map-a-Planet Web Map Service
  • image/jpeg, image/jpg, image/gif, image/tiff, image/pds, image/raw
  • 12 layers

Question 5 (for one of the layers in service 1):

  • What is the name of the layer?
  • What is the SRS of the layer?
  • What is the name of the projection that matches the SRS EPSG code?
  • What is the LatLonBoundingBox of the layer?
  • mola_slope
  • IAU2000:49900. Also supports EPSG:4326.
  • Mars 2000
  • minx="-180" miny="-90" maxx="180" maxy="90"
